## Revitalize Your Headwear: How To Clean Hats In The Bathtub With Borax

Is your favorite hat looking a little worse for wear? Don't despair! You don't necessarily need to take it to a professional cleaner. Cleaning hats at home can be surprisingly easy, and using Borax in your bathtub is a fantastic, effective method. This article will guide you through the process of safely and effectively cleaning your hats, restoring them to their former glory. We’ll cover everything from preparing your hat to the final drying steps, ensuring your headwear stays looking its best.

**Takeaway:**

*   Borax is a gentle yet powerful cleaning agent perfect for hats.
*   Handwashing in the bathtub prevents damage from washing machines.
*   Proper drying is crucial to maintain the hat’s shape.

**Can you wash a hat in the bathtub?**

Yes, you can! Washing a hat in the bathtub is a gentle and effective way to clean it, especially when using a mild cleaning agent like Borax. It allows for focused cleaning and prevents the hat from being tossed around in a washing machine, which can damage its shape.

## Why Use Borax to Clean Hats?

Borax, also known as sodium borate, is a naturally occurring mineral with amazing cleaning properties. It’s a fantastic alternative to harsh chemicals and is surprisingly gentle on fabrics. Using Borax to clean hats helps remove dirt, sweat, and odors without causing significant fading or damage. It also acts as a natural deodorizer, leaving your hat smelling fresh. ## Preparing Your Hat for a Bathtub Clean

Before you jump into cleaning, a little preparation goes a long way. First, check the hat’s care label. While Borax is generally safe, some delicate materials might require professional cleaning.  If the label allows for handwashing, you’re good to go! Next, remove any detachable decorations or bands. Brush off any loose dirt or debris with a soft-bristled brush.  Finally, spot-treat any particularly stubborn stains with a diluted Borax solution before proceeding to the full bath.

## The Bathtub Cleaning Process: Step-by-Step

Now for the main event! Fill your bathtub with lukewarm water. Add about 1/2 cup of Borax to the water and stir until it’s completely dissolved. Submerge your hat in the Borax solution, gently pressing it down to ensure it’s fully saturated. Let the hat soak for about 30 minutes. This allows the Borax to loosen dirt and grime. After soaking, gently agitate the hat in the water, focusing on areas that are particularly dirty. Avoid harsh scrubbing, which can damage the fabric. ## Rinsing and Removing Excess Water

Once you’ve cleaned the hat, it’s time to rinse away the Borax solution. Drain the bathtub and refill it with clean, lukewarm water. Gently rinse the hat until all traces of Borax are gone. Avoid wringing or twisting the hat, as this can distort its shape. Instead, gently press out excess water. You can also use a clean towel to blot the hat, absorbing as much moisture as possible.

## Drying Your Hat Properly

Proper drying is crucial to maintaining your hat’s shape and preventing mildew. Never put your hat in the dryer! The heat can cause it to shrink or become misshapen. Instead, reshape the hat to its original form. You can use a hat form, a balloon, or even a rolled-up towel stuffed inside to help maintain the shape. Place the hat in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or heat. Allow it to air dry completely, which may take several hours or even overnight. ## Cleaning Different Hat Materials with Borax

Borax is versatile, but some materials require extra care. 

*   **Cotton Hats:** These are generally the most durable and can withstand a more vigorous cleaning.
*   **Wool Hats:** Wool is more delicate. Use a gentler touch and avoid prolonged soaking.
*   **Straw Hats:** Straw hats are particularly susceptible to damage. Use a very diluted Borax solution and avoid getting the straw too wet.
*   **Felt Hats:** Felt hats require careful handling. Spot clean whenever possible and avoid submerging them completely.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Hats with Borax

**Q: Is Borax safe for all hat colors?**

A: Borax is generally safe for most colors, but it’s always a good idea to test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn’t cause any fading.

**Q: Can I use Borax on vintage hats?**

A: Vintage hats are often delicate. It’s best to consult a professional cleaner for valuable or fragile vintage hats.

**Q: How often should I clean my hats?**

A: The frequency depends on how often you wear your hats. Generally, cleaning them every few months or as needed will keep them looking their best.

**Q: What if my hat still smells after cleaning?**

A: Ensure you’ve thoroughly rinsed all the Borax solution from the hat. You can also sprinkle a small amount of baking soda inside the hat and let it sit overnight to absorb any lingering odors.
