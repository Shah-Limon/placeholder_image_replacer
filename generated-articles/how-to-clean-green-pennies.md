## How to Clean Green Pennies: A Step-by-Step Guide

Have you ever wondered how to bring back the luster to those dull, green pennies? It’s a common problem – copper naturally tarnishes over time, creating that greenish patina. Don't worry, restoring your pennies doesn't require harsh chemicals or a lot of effort. This article will walk you through several effective methods to clean green pennies, from gentle household solutions to more targeted approaches. We’ll cover everything you need to know to safely remove tarnish and reveal the copper shine beneath.

**Takeaway:**

*   Use gentle methods like vinegar and salt for most pennies.
*   Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the surface.
*   Rinse thoroughly and dry completely to prevent re-tarnishing.
*   Consider the coin's value before cleaning, as cleaning can sometimes decrease collectible value.

**What’s the best way to clean green pennies?**

The best way to clean green pennies involves a simple solution of vinegar, salt, and water. Soak the pennies for a few minutes, then gently scrub and rinse. This method effectively removes tarnish without damaging the copper.

## Understanding Why Pennies Turn Green

Before diving into cleaning methods, it’s helpful to understand *why* pennies turn green. The green color isn’t actually corrosion, but a patina formed by a chemical reaction between the copper, oxygen, and other elements like sulfur in the air. This process is natural and protects the copper from further degradation. However, many people prefer the bright shine of clean copper.  Understanding this process helps you choose the right cleaning method. ## The Vinegar and Salt Method: A Classic Solution

The vinegar and salt method is a popular and effective way to clean green pennies. It’s gentle enough for most pennies and uses ingredients you likely already have at home. Here’s how to do it:

1.  **Mix the Solution:** Combine 1 tablespoon of salt with 1/2 cup of white vinegar in a non-metallic bowl.
2.  **Soak the Pennies:** Add the green pennies to the solution, ensuring they are fully submerged.
3.  **Let it Sit:** Allow the pennies to soak for 15-30 minutes, depending on the level of tarnish.
4.  **Gentle Scrubbing:** Remove the pennies and gently scrub them with a soft cloth or toothbrush.
5.  **Rinse and Dry:** Rinse the pennies thoroughly with water and dry them completely with a clean towel.

This method works because the vinegar’s acidity helps dissolve the copper oxide, while the salt acts as a mild abrasive.

## Lemon Juice and Baking Soda: A Natural Alternative

If you prefer a more natural cleaning solution, lemon juice and baking soda are a great option. Lemon juice contains citric acid, which works similarly to vinegar, and baking soda provides a gentle abrasive action. 

1.  **Create a Paste:** Mix lemon juice with enough baking soda to form a paste.
2.  **Apply to Pennies:** Apply the paste to the green pennies, ensuring they are coated.
3.  **Let it Sit:** Allow the paste to sit for 10-15 minutes.
4.  **Gentle Scrubbing:** Gently scrub the pennies with a soft cloth or toothbrush.
5.  **Rinse and Dry:** Rinse the pennies thoroughly with water and dry them completely.

This method is particularly good for lightly tarnished pennies.  For tougher grime on other surfaces, you might consider [how to clean a copper still](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-a-copper-still) using similar gentle techniques.

## Using Ketchup: Surprisingly Effective!

Believe it or not, ketchup can be an effective penny cleaner! The acidity in tomatoes helps break down the tarnish. 

1.  **Coat the Pennies:** Cover the green pennies with a layer of ketchup.
2.  **Let it Sit:** Allow the ketchup to sit for 30 minutes to an hour.
3.  **Rinse and Dry:** Rinse the pennies thoroughly with water and dry them completely.

This method is surprisingly effective, especially for pennies with moderate tarnish.

## Commercial Penny Cleaners: When to Use Them

While homemade solutions are often preferred, commercial penny cleaners are available. These cleaners typically contain stronger chemicals and can be more effective for heavily tarnished pennies. However, use them with caution, as they can be abrasive and potentially damage the coins. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully. ## Preventing Future Tarnishing

Once you’ve cleaned your pennies, you’ll want to prevent them from tarnishing again. Here are a few tips:

*   **Store in an Airtight Container:** Store the cleaned pennies in an airtight container or plastic bag to minimize exposure to air and moisture.
*   **Silica Gel Packets:** Include a few silica gel packets in the container to absorb any remaining moisture.
*   **Handle with Clean Hands:** Avoid handling the pennies with bare hands, as the oils from your skin can contribute to tarnishing.
*   **Regular Polishing:** Periodically polish the pennies with a soft cloth to maintain their shine.

## Important Considerations: Coin Value and Cleaning

Before you start cleaning, it’s important to consider the coin’s value. While cleaning can improve the appearance of a penny, it can also *decrease* its collectible value. Collectors often prefer coins with their original patina. If you’re unsure about the value of your pennies, it’s best to consult with a coin expert before cleaning them. ## FAQ About Cleaning Green Pennies

**Q: Will cleaning pennies damage them?**

A: Gentle cleaning methods like vinegar and salt are generally safe. However, abrasive cleaners or excessive scrubbing can scratch the surface. Always test a small area first.

**Q: Can I use toothpaste to clean pennies?**

A: While some people recommend toothpaste, it can be abrasive and potentially damage the coins. It’s best to stick to gentler methods.

**Q: How long does it take to clean green pennies?**

A: The cleaning time depends on the level of tarnish. Lightly tarnished pennies may only require a 10-15 minute soak, while heavily tarnished pennies may need an hour or more.

**Q: Is it safe to mix vinegar and baking soda?**

A: While they neutralize each other, it's safe to mix them, but the resulting fizzing action reduces their individual cleaning power. It's more effective to use them separately.
