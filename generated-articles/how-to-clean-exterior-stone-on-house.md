## Revitalize Your Home: How to Clean Exterior Stone on House

Is your home’s stone exterior looking a little dull? Over time, dirt, algae, and other debris can accumulate, diminishing the natural beauty of your stone. Cleaning exterior stone isn’t just about aesthetics; it’s about protecting your investment and preventing potential damage. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to safely and effectively clean the stone on your house, leaving it looking its best. We’ll cover different stone types, cleaning methods, and preventative measures to keep your home looking beautiful for years to come.

**Takeaway:**

*   Identify your stone type for appropriate cleaning.
*   Start with gentle methods before resorting to power washing.
*   Use pH-neutral cleaners specifically designed for stone.
*   Protect yourself with appropriate safety gear.
*   Regular maintenance prevents buildup and simplifies cleaning.

Cleaning exterior stone requires a bit of knowledge and care. Here's a quick answer: To clean exterior stone, identify the stone type, gently scrub with a pH-neutral cleaner and soft brush, rinse thoroughly, and consider a sealant for protection.

## Understanding Your Stone: Identification is Key

Before you begin, it’s crucial to identify the type of stone your house is clad in. Different stones require different cleaning approaches. Using the wrong method can cause damage, discoloration, or etching. Common types of exterior house stone include limestone, sandstone, granite, slate, and brownstone. 

*   **Limestone:** A softer stone, susceptible to acid damage.
*   **Sandstone:** Porous and can stain easily.
*   **Granite:** Very durable and resistant to most cleaners.
*   **Slate:** Can be prone to flaking if improperly cleaned.
*   **Brownstone:** A type of sandstone, often found on older homes.

If you’re unsure of your stone type, consult a professional mason or stone specialist. Knowing your stone will guide your choice of cleaning solutions and techniques. ## Gentle Cleaning: The First Line of Defense

Often, a gentle approach is all you need to restore your stone’s appearance. Start with the least abrasive method possible. This involves using a soft-bristled brush, warm water, and a pH-neutral cleaner specifically formulated for stone. Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach, vinegar, or ammonia, as these can damage many types of stone. 

*   Mix the cleaner with water according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
*   Apply the solution to the stone surface.
*   Gently scrub with the brush, working in circular motions.
*   Rinse thoroughly with clean water.

This method is effective for removing light dirt, dust, and grime. It’s also a good starting point for any stone type. If you're dealing with stubborn dirt, consider a slightly longer soaking time before scrubbing.

## Power Washing: When to Use It (and How to Do It Safely)

Power washing can be an effective way to clean exterior stone, but it must be done with caution. High pressure can damage softer stones like limestone and sandstone. If you choose to power wash, use a wide-angle nozzle and a low-pressure setting. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure the stone can withstand the pressure.

*   Maintain a safe distance from the stone surface (at least 12 inches).
*   Move the nozzle in a consistent, sweeping motion.
*   Avoid concentrating the spray on one spot for too long.
*   Rinse thoroughly with clean water.

## Tackling Specific Stains: Algae, Mold, and More

Sometimes, gentle cleaning isn’t enough to remove stubborn stains. Algae and mold are common culprits, especially in damp climates. For these types of stains, you can use a specialized stone cleaner designed to kill algae and mold. Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully.

*   **Oil stains:** Absorb with a poultice made of baking soda and water.
*   **Rust stains:** Use a commercial rust remover specifically for stone.
*   **Bird droppings:** Remove promptly with warm water and a soft brush.

Remember to test any cleaning solution in an inconspicuous area before applying it to the entire surface. If you're struggling with persistent stains, consider consulting a professional stone restoration company. ### Choosing the Right Cleaning Products

Selecting the right cleaning product is paramount. Avoid anything acidic or abrasive. Look for cleaners specifically labeled for use on natural stone. pH-neutral options are generally the safest bet. Read labels carefully and follow the manufacturer’s instructions. Consider eco-friendly cleaning solutions to minimize your environmental impact.

## Protecting Your Investment: Sealing Your Stone

Once your stone is clean, consider applying a sealant. Sealants help protect the stone from water damage, staining, and weathering. They also make future cleaning easier. There are different types of sealants available, including penetrating sealants and topical sealants.

*   **Penetrating sealants:** Soak into the stone, providing long-lasting protection without altering the appearance.
*   **Topical sealants:** Form a protective layer on the surface, enhancing the stone’s color and sheen.

The type of sealant you choose will depend on your stone type and desired level of protection. Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions for application.

## Maintaining Clean Stone: Preventative Measures

Regular maintenance is the key to keeping your exterior stone looking its best. Simple preventative measures can significantly reduce the need for deep cleaning.

*   **Regularly sweep or brush the stone surface to remove loose dirt and debris.**
*   **Trim nearby vegetation to prevent moisture buildup.**
*   **Promptly clean up spills and stains.**
*   **Inspect the sealant annually and reapply as needed.**

By following these tips, you can protect your investment and enjoy the beauty of your stone exterior for years to come. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use bleach to clean my exterior stone?**
A: No, bleach can damage many types of stone, causing discoloration and etching. It’s best to use a pH-neutral cleaner specifically designed for stone.

**Q: How often should I clean my exterior stone?**
A: This depends on your climate and the amount of dirt and debris your stone is exposed to. Generally, cleaning once or twice a year is sufficient.

**Q: What’s the best way to remove moss from stone?**
A: Gently scrape off the moss with a plastic scraper, then clean the area with a stone cleaner designed to kill moss.

**Q: Is it safe to use a pressure washer on sandstone?**
A: Sandstone is a relatively soft stone, so using a pressure washer is risky. If you must use one, use a very low-pressure setting and a wide-angle nozzle.

**Q: How do I know if my stone is sealed?**
A: You can test this by dropping a few drops of water onto the stone. If the water beads up, the stone is sealed. If the water soaks in, it needs to be resealed.
