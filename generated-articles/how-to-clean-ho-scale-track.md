## Keeping Your Trains Rolling: How To Clean HO Scale Track

Is your HO scale train experiencing frustrating stops and starts? Dirty track is often the culprit! Maintaining clean track is essential for reliable operation and enjoying your model railroad. This article will guide you through the most effective methods for cleaning your HO scale track, from simple solutions to more advanced techniques. We’ll cover everything you need to know to keep your trains running smoothly and prevent those annoying derailments. Let's dive in and get your railway back on track!

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular cleaning prevents corrosion and debris buildup.
*   Use dedicated track cleaning solutions for best results.
*   Multiple methods exist, from simple wiping to specialized cars.
*   Prevention is key – protect your track from dust and oxidation.

**Quick Answer:**

Cleaning HO scale track involves removing dust, dirt, and oxidation. You can use track cleaning cars, rubbing alcohol with a clean cloth, or specialized track cleaning solutions. Regular cleaning ensures good electrical contact for reliable train operation.

## Why Cleaning Your HO Scale Track Matters

Dirty track creates resistance, hindering the electrical current needed to power your locomotives. This resistance leads to intermittent power, causing trains to slow down, stall, or even derail. Dust, dirt, and oxidation (a natural process that forms on metal) all contribute to this problem. Regularly cleaning your track isn’t just about aesthetics; it’s about ensuring consistent and reliable performance. A clean track means a happier model railroader!

## Simple Cleaning Methods for HO Scale Track

Sometimes, a quick clean is all you need. These methods are great for light dust and debris. 

*   **Clean Cloth and Rubbing Alcohol:** Dampen a lint-free cloth with 91% isopropyl alcohol. Gently wipe the rails, ensuring you cover the entire surface. Avoid using paper towels, as they can leave behind lint.
*   **Track Cleaning Eraser:** These specialized erasers gently abrade the rail surface, removing oxidation and grime. Use light pressure and follow the direction of the track.
*   **Dedicated Track Cleaning Fluid:** Many brands offer track cleaning fluids specifically designed for model railroads. Apply with a cloth or a track cleaning applicator. ## Utilizing Track Cleaning Cars

Track cleaning cars are a convenient and effective way to clean your entire layout. These cars typically have a cleaning pad or brush that scrubs the rails as the car is pulled or pushed by a locomotive. 

*   **Types of Cleaning Cars:** Options range from simple pad-style cars to more advanced models with built-in cleaning fluids.
*   **Using a Cleaning Car:** Attach the cleaning car to the rear of a locomotive and run it around your layout. Ensure the cleaning pad is clean and properly moistened with track cleaning fluid.
*   **Maintenance:** Regularly clean or replace the cleaning pad to maintain effectiveness.

## Deep Cleaning: Addressing Stubborn Oxidation

Sometimes, simple cleaning isn’t enough. Stubborn oxidation requires a more aggressive approach.

*   **Bright Boy:** A Bright Boy is a polishing tool that physically polishes the rails, removing heavy oxidation. Use with caution, as excessive use can wear down the rails.
*   **Steel Wool (Fine Grade):** Very fine steel wool (0000 grade) can be used to gently polish the rails. Be extremely careful to remove all steel wool particles afterward, as they can cause shorts.
*   **Track Cleaning Solutions with Additives:** Some track cleaning solutions contain mild abrasives to help remove stubborn grime. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions. ## Preventing Track From Getting Dirty

Prevention is always better than cure. Taking steps to protect your track from dirt and oxidation will reduce the frequency of cleaning.

*   **Layout Enclosure:** Enclosing your layout helps keep dust and debris out. A simple cover or dedicated room can make a big difference.
*   **Anti-Oxidation Products:** Apply a thin coat of anti-oxidation product to the rails to help prevent corrosion.
*   **Regular Dusting:** Regularly dust your layout to remove loose particles before they settle on the track.
*   **Storage:** When storing track, keep it in a clean, dry place to prevent oxidation.

## Cleaning Turnouts and Switches

Turnouts and switches are particularly prone to dirt buildup due to their moving parts. Pay special attention to these areas when cleaning.

*   **Pointed Tools:** Use a small brush or cotton swab to clean around the points of the turnout.
*   **Cleaning Fluid Application:** Apply track cleaning fluid directly to the turnout and work it into the moving parts.

## Troubleshooting Track Cleaning Issues

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, you may encounter issues. Here are some common problems and solutions.

*   **Short Circuits:** If you experience short circuits after cleaning, it’s likely due to residual cleaning fluid or debris. Run a clean locomotive slowly around the layout to identify the location of the short.
*   **Still Dirty Track:** If the track remains dirty after cleaning, you may need to use a more aggressive cleaning method or replace the track.
*   **Locomotive Stalling:** If locomotives continue to stall, check the locomotive's wheels and pickups for dirt or corrosion. Consider cleaning the wheels with a track cleaning pad. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I clean my HO scale track?**
A: It depends on your layout environment and usage. Generally, cleaning every few weeks or months is sufficient. However, if you notice performance issues, clean the track immediately.

**Q: Can I use household cleaners on my HO scale track?**
A: Avoid using harsh household cleaners, as they can damage the track and locomotive components. Stick to dedicated track cleaning solutions or isopropyl alcohol.

**Q: What's the best way to remove rust from HO scale track?**
A: Fine steel wool (0000 grade) can remove rust, but use it cautiously. A Bright Boy is also effective for removing rust and oxidation.

**Q: Is it safe to use water to clean my HO scale track?**
A: Avoid using water directly on the track, as it can cause corrosion. If you must use water, ensure the track is completely dry before running trains.

**Q: What do I do with the residue after cleaning?**
A: Dispose of used cleaning cloths and pads properly. Avoid pouring cleaning fluids down the drain; follow local regulations for disposal.
