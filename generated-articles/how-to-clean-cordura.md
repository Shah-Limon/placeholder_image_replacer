## How To Clean Cordura: A Complete Guide

Is your Cordura gear looking a little worse for wear? Don't worry, this durable fabric is surprisingly easy to clean and maintain. Cordura is known for its strength and resistance to abrasions, making it ideal for backpacks, luggage, and outdoor equipment. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to effectively clean Cordura, keeping your favorite items in top condition. We’ll cover everything from quick spot treatments to thorough deep cleans, ensuring your gear looks great and lasts longer.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly brush off dirt and debris.
*   Spot clean stains immediately with mild soap and water.
*   For deep cleaning, use a soft brush, mild detergent, and lukewarm water.
*   Air dry completely, avoiding direct sunlight or heat.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean Cordura, start by brushing off loose dirt. Then, mix mild soap with lukewarm water and gently scrub the fabric with a soft brush. Rinse thoroughly and allow to air dry completely, away from direct heat.

## Understanding Cordura Fabric

Cordura is a family of textured nylon fabrics known for their exceptional durability and resistance to tears and abrasions. It’s often used in products that need to withstand tough conditions, like hiking backpacks and workwear. Different weights and weaves of Cordura exist, but the core principle remains the same: a robust, long-lasting material. Knowing this helps you understand *how* to clean it effectively. 

The fabric’s tightly woven structure makes it relatively resistant to staining, but dirt and grime can still accumulate over time. Regular cleaning prevents buildup and maintains the fabric’s protective qualities. ## Spot Cleaning Cordura: Addressing Fresh Stains

Addressing stains quickly is key to preventing them from setting into the Cordura fabric. For fresh spills or marks, act fast! Begin by blotting the stain with a clean, damp cloth to remove any excess liquid or debris. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the stain and potentially damage the fabric. 

Next, create a mild cleaning solution by mixing a small amount of gentle liquid soap (like dish soap) with lukewarm water. Dip a clean cloth or soft brush into the solution and gently dab at the stain. Work from the outside in to prevent spreading. Once the stain is lifted, rinse the area with clean water and blot dry with a fresh cloth. For stubborn stains, you might need to repeat the process.

## Deep Cleaning Cordura: A Thorough Approach

Sometimes, spot cleaning isn’t enough, and your Cordura gear needs a more thorough cleaning. This is especially true for items that have been exposed to significant dirt, mud, or prolonged use. Start by emptying the item completely and removing any detachable parts. 

Fill a basin or sink with lukewarm water and add a small amount of mild detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals or bleach, as these can damage the fabric. Submerge a soft brush or cloth into the soapy water and gently scrub the Cordura surface. Pay attention to areas that are particularly dirty or stained. Rinse thoroughly with clean water until all traces of soap are gone. ## Drying Cordura Properly: Avoiding Damage

Proper drying is crucial to prevent mildew and maintain the integrity of your Cordura fabric. Never put Cordura in a dryer, as the heat can cause shrinkage or damage the fabric’s coating. Instead, allow the item to air dry completely in a well-ventilated area. 

Avoid direct sunlight, as prolonged exposure can fade the color of the fabric. If possible, hang the item on a clothesline or lay it flat on a clean surface. Ensure the item is completely dry before storing it away. This prevents the growth of mold and mildew, extending the life of your Cordura gear.

## Maintaining Cordura: Preventative Measures

Preventative care is the best way to keep your Cordura gear looking its best and reduce the need for frequent deep cleaning. Regularly brush off loose dirt and debris after each use. This simple step prevents dirt from becoming embedded in the fabric. 

Consider using a fabric protector spray specifically designed for nylon or Cordura. These sprays create a barrier that repels water and stains, making cleaning easier in the long run. Store your Cordura items in a cool, dry place when not in use. ## Dealing with Specific Stains on Cordura

Different types of stains require slightly different approaches. Here’s a quick guide to tackling some common culprits:

*   **Oil-based stains:** Sprinkle baking soda on the stain to absorb the oil. Let it sit for several hours, then brush it off. Follow with a spot cleaning using mild soap and water.
*   **Mud:** Allow the mud to dry completely, then brush it off. If a stain remains, spot clean with mild soap and water.
*   **Ink:** Dab the stain with rubbing alcohol using a clean cloth. Blot gently and repeat until the ink is lifted.
*   **Grease:** Similar to oil-based stains, use baking soda to absorb the grease before spot cleaning.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I use bleach on Cordura?**

A: No, bleach can damage the fabric and cause discoloration. Always use mild detergent and lukewarm water for cleaning Cordura.

**Q: How often should I deep clean my Cordura gear?**

A: It depends on how frequently you use it and the conditions it’s exposed to. Generally, deep cleaning every 3-6 months is sufficient for regular use.

**Q: Will cleaning affect the water resistance of Cordura?**

A: Cleaning can temporarily reduce water resistance. Reapply a fabric protector spray after cleaning to restore its water-repellent properties.

**Q: Is it safe to machine wash Cordura?**

A: While some Cordura items *can* be machine washed, it’s generally not recommended. Hand washing is the safest option to prevent damage. If you do machine wash, use a gentle cycle, cold water, and a mild detergent, and always air dry.
